Overview - Problem Statement

Processing monthly enterprise telecommunication invoices can be a labor-intensive and error-prone task, especially when dealing with dozens or hundreds of mobile lines. Telekom invoices are typically provided in PDF format, containing a dense mix of service fees, usage charges, and various VAT rates (e.g., 27% and 5%).

Before this project, the manual data entry and cross-checking of a single monthly invoice took approximately 2 hours of focused administrative work. This manual process was not only slow but carried a high risk of human error. The goal was to eliminate this bottleneck by creating a script that performs the same task with 100% accuracy in a fraction of the time.

Development process

The development focused on high-speed data extraction and automated categorization. I implemented a robust PDF parsing layer using the PyMuPDF (fitz) library to access the digital text layer directly.

As shown in the attached invoice image, a major technical hurdle was the significant text fragmentation within the PDF. These broken text segments caused initial challenges during the extraction process, requiring a sophisticated state-machine parser and complex string-cleaning logic to ensure data integrity.

Development process

Beyond the technical extraction, the invoice presented diverse data structures for different types of services: standard mobile phone numbers, dedicated data cards (M2M), and additional services like mobile parking fees. I designed a unified data extraction framework capable of identifying and normalizing these varied structures into a consistent, structured dataset. By using Regular Expressions (Regex) and Pandas, the system automatically maps each item to its respective TESZOR code, automating a complex accounting workflow that was previously handled manually.

Results & Impact

The implementation of the telekom_szamlafeldolgozo.py script resulted in a massive efficiency gain, transforming a once-tedious administrative burden into a near-instantaneous process. While the manual processing of a single invoice previously required roughly 120 minutes of focused labor, the automated script completes the entire task in approximately 0.8 seconds. This represents an efficiency increase of roughly 9,000 times, while simultaneously eliminating the possibility of human typing errors or calculation oversights, ensuring perfect data reliability for the finance department.
